For Matilde's Reference

Caption Style Guide

This is the starting point — a quick reference Matilde can use before writing any caption. A full brand guidelines doc will be built out as a companion resource. The core idea: Freestyle captions are bold, fun, and direct — they feel like a confident friend who snacks unapologetically.

📌 Full brand guidelines link to be added here once the style guide is written. For now, use this as your north star and reference past posts when in doubt.

✓ Do This

  • Short, punchy opening line that stops the scroll
  • Lead with the vibe, not the product specs
  • Use real use-case language — "pickle on a hot dog," "martini night," "afternoon snack break"
  • Emojis used with intention — not every sentence
  • First person plural is fine: "we," "our" for brand posts
"Pickles for breakfast? We don't make the rules. 🥒 "

✕ Avoid This

  • Long paragraphs — if it needs a "more" tap to read, it's too long
  • Generic openers: "We are so excited to share…"
  • Over-explaining the product — let it speak for itself
  • All-caps for emphasis (use it for brand moments only, sparingly)
  • More than 5–6 hashtags
  • Salesy language: "Buy now!" "Don't miss out!" "Limited time!"
  • Filler phrases: "Tag a friend who…", "Drop a 🔥 if…"
"We are SO excited to announce our NEW Bread & Butter Pickle Chips flavor!! 🎉🎉 These are now available on our website — click the link in bio to shop now before they sell out!! Don't miss out!! #snacks #pickles #healthysnacks #glutenfree #snacktime #foodie #picklechips #newproduct"
